Bioinformatics . Computational biology · Multi-omics · Systems biology

I am a bioinformatics scientist and a computational biologist developing integrative computational methods and models to elucidate how the host genome, microbiome, and molecular environment interact to shape human health, particularly gastrointestinal diseases. My research combines multi-omic integration, machine learning, and network-based modeling to identify biomarkers, molecular signatures, and testable mechanisms across large-scale clinical cohorts and multi-modal high-throughput molecular data. My goal is to translate these computational discoveries into precision diagnostics, patient stratification strategies, and therapeutic targets that improve outcomes for patients, advance disease prevention, and promote long-term population health.

The human gut is a dynamic ecosystem whose disruption contributes to some of the most prevalent and poorly understood diseases of our time, including inflammatory bowel disease, colorectal cancer, metabolic disorders, and functional gastrointestinal conditions. Host genetic factors alone cannot explain the rapid rise in many of these diseases, implicating environmental exposures and the microbiome as central contributors to disease susceptibility and progression. Yet much of microbiome research remains associational, leaving unresolved how microbial communities mechanistically influence disease pathobiology.

Health depends on coordinated communication within and between host and microbial systems. Within the host, molecular networks regulate immune function, epithelial integrity, metabolism, and tissue repair. Within the microbiome, microbial genes, strains, metabolites, and community interactions shape ecological stability and functional output. Disease may emerge when these systems become disrupted through genetic variation, altered signaling, microbial adaptation, environmental perturbation, or miscommunication across the host–microbiome interface.

My work sits at this intersection. I develop computational approaches to model microbiome dynamics across individuals, disease states, and therapeutic contexts, with the goal of identifying microbial features, molecular pathways, and host–microbe interactions that explain disease progression, treatment response, and clinically relevant phenotypes. Ultimately, my independent research program would pair computational discovery with experimental systems, including organoids and host–microbe models, to evaluate mechanisms and advance microbiome-informed strategies for prediction, diagnosis, and precision medicine.
