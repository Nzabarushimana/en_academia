Core research areas

My research program centers on computational modeling, multi-omic and multi-modal systems biology, and interpretable machine learning to study biological complexity.

01

Computational modeling of complex biological systems

Developing models that represent biological variation across molecular, microbial, host, and clinical scales, with an emphasis on dynamic host–microbiome systems.

02

Multi-omic and multi-modal data integration and systems biology

Integrating microbial, host, clinical, and experimental datasets to characterize microbiome dynamics and connect biological variation with function.

03

Interpretable machine learning for biological data

Building transparent computational models that connect high-dimensional biological data with mechanisms, disease progression, therapeutic response, and patient stratification.

Microbial ecology and community dynamics

Studying how microbial communities vary across individuals, environments, and disease states.

Evolutionary genomics and functional adaptation

Investigating strain variation, lateral gene transfer, genome evolution, and microbial adaptation.

Precision medicine and translational data science

Advancing microbiome-informed strategies for prediction, diagnosis, therapeutic response, and risk stratification.
