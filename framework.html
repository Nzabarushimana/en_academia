Research vision

My research develops computational methods for modeling complex biological systems, with a primary focus on the human microbiome and gastrointestinal diseases. I integrate algorithmic thinking, interpretable machine learning, multi-omic data integration, and systems biology to elucidate how microbial ecosystems interact with host molecular networks. My long-term vision is to pair computational discovery with experimental validation in organoids, host–microbe models, and perturbation-based systems to identify mechanisms, biomarkers, and microbiome-informed strategies for precision medicine.

Methods

Computational modeling

Algorithmic thinking, statistical learning, interpretable models, and reproducible data integration.

Data

Multi-omic and multi-modal systems biology

Metagenomic, metatranscriptomic, metabolomic, microbial genomic, clinical, and experimental data.

Primary system Host–microbiome dynamics
Biology

Mechanistic inference

Microbial ecology, evolutionary genomics, community dynamics, and host–microbe interactions.

Impact

Precision microbiome medicine

Disease susceptibility, risk prediction, diagnosis, patient stratification, therapeutic response, prevention, and targeted experimental validation.
